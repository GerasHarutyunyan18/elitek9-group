Working Dogs Need Working Dog Nutrition

A working dog's nutritional needs differ significantly from a typical pet. High activity, training stress, and athletic demands require specialized nutrition.

Key Nutritional Principles:

1. High-Quality Protein (28-32%)

Protein builds and repairs muscle. Look for named meat sources as the first ingredient: chicken, beef, lamb, or fish.

2. Moderate Fat (15-20%)

Fat provides sustained energy. Working dogs burn calories quickly and need healthy fats like chicken fat or fish oil.

3. Digestible Carbohydrates

Rice, oats, and sweet potatoes provide quick energy without upsetting the stomach.

4. Joint Support

Glucosamine and chondroitin support joint health, crucial for athletic dogs.

Feeding Schedule for Working Dogs:

  • Feed 2-3 times daily (never right before training)
  • Train at least 2 hours after meals
  • Provide fresh water at all times
  • Adjust portions based on activity level

What to Avoid:

  • Grain-free diets (linked to heart issues)
  • Excessive treats (no more than 10% of daily calories)
  • Table scraps and human food
  • Cheap foods with fillers and by-products

Our Recommendation

We feed our working dogs premium performance formulas designed for active breeds. Brands we trust include Purina Pro Plan Sport, Royal Canin Working Dog, and Eukanuba Premium Performance.

Remember: every dog is different. Work with your vet to find the perfect nutrition plan for your working partner.
